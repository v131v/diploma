## Abstract

Erasure Coding (EC) in cloud storage minimizes [data replication](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/computer-science/data-replication) by reconstructing data from parity fragments. This method enhances [data redundancy](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/computer-science/data-redundancy) and efficiency while reducing storage costs and improving [fault tolerance](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/computer-science/fault-tolerance). It is more advantageous than replication in Object Storage Systems. EC guarantees data integrity by ensuring lossless transmission of all coded pieces. As data volumes continue to increase rapidly, the time efficiency of the EC method becomes crucial in ensuring optimal system performance. Various variables, including the algorithm employed, data size, number of storage nodes, hardware resources, and network conditions, can influence the speed of EC operations. Although some literature covers various aspects, there is still a research gap in understanding the I/O activities, time efficiency, and [fault tolerance](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/computer-science/fault-tolerance) of EC in object storage systems. Hence, our research aims to address these challenges in cloud-based object storage systems. We analyze and benchmark the data storage I/O performance of OpenStack Swift, focusing on the time efficiency of the Reed–Solomon (RS) algorithm across two datasets. Additionally, our contributions include benchmarking [EC performance](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/computer-science/coding-performance) in both local and remote [testbeds](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/computer-science/testbed), utilizing the SimEDC simulator for comprehensive efficiency and fault tolerance assessments. Moreover, we create a comprehensive dataset (MCSD-100) for benchmarking and conduct a systematic literature review. Finally, we identify and discuss future opportunities for enhancing EC in cloud-based object storage systems.

## Introduction

Distributed Storage Systems (DSS) are critical in managing the immense challenges posed by the exponential growth of data. They provide scalable and reliable data storage solutions [1], [2]. Integrating Erasure Coding [3] into DSS is essential as it facilitates efficient data distribution, minimizes storage overhead, ensures robust data durability, and strengthens fault tolerance. EC thus stands out as a robust choice for empowering large-scale storage systems, offering enhanced reliability and efficiency in managing modern data demands. It addresses shortcomings of traditional methods by improving time efficiency, user-friendliness, and fault tolerance. EC distributes data across multiple devices, creating redundant fragments that ensure data integrity even in the event of device failures, thereby mitigating the risk of data loss.

Additionally, EC’s reduced storage overhead enhances system performance and user experience by providing redundancy in a more compact form. Its seamless data recovery eliminates the need for extensive system modifications or user training, ensuring ease of use. Evaluating EC scheme performance is critical for informed decision-making and advancing data storage technologies. EC holds significant promise in reducing redundant storage requirements and addressing challenges related to fault tolerance and time efficiency. Research into EC extends beyond basic comprehension, focusing on benchmarking erasure codes with a strong emphasis on data security and various benchmarking factors to assess their effectiveness.

Besides, researchers have explored various solution-based approaches to enhance the performance and effectiveness of EC techniques. Chiniah et al. [3] assess the integration of Erasure Codes in popular distributed storage systems, covering design principles, data models, failure handling, consistency, and security. Greenan et al. [4] investigate using Erasure Codes to reduce power consumption in storage systems through power-aware coding techniques. Balaji et al. [5] examine efficient repair methods for distributed storage, including regenerating codes and locally recoverable codes. Wang et al. [6] propose a secure integrity auditing mechanism for cloud storage using distributed erasure-coded data and homomorphic tokens. Dong et al. [7] introduce Coop-U, a cooperative update scheme for enhancing the performance of erasure-coded data updates in distributed storage systems.

Moreover, Lin et al. [8] propose a secure decentralized erasure code combining threshold public key encryption and a variant of decentralized erasure code to address privacy concerns in distributed networked storage systems. The resulting system ensures robustness, privacy, and cost-effectiveness, safeguarding data even if all storage servers are compromised. Pei et al. [9] introduce Group-U, a grouped and pipelined update scheme for erasure-coded storage systems, significantly reducing update overhead and time compared to existing methods. This approach optimizes efficiency by dynamically adjusting group size and optimizing data transmission and computation. While some studies benchmark EC in terms of cost analysis [10], failure repair [11], storage efficiency [10], [12], [13], data availability, durability [14], and fault tolerance [13], [14], none specifically focus on optimizing time efficiency in terms of I/O operations for EC schemes in Object Storage System.

Benchmarking EC is essential for evaluating performance, scalability, and optimization, enabling comparative analyses and standardization. It provides insights into operational efficiency, identifies areas for improvement, and aids in selecting suitable solutions. EC is widely adopted across various domains: ensuring data durability and cost-effectiveness in cloud storage, enhancing fault tolerance and scalability in distributed file systems, optimizing storage efficiency in backups and archives, and supporting resource utilization in large-scale systems. It also ensures high availability in data centers and reliable data storage in IoT applications, offering a valuable alternative to replication in handling today’s data growth challenges [3]. Organizations use benchmarking to assess system performance across different workloads, compare various implementations, and contribute to benchmark standardization.

Erasure Coding has become a promising solution for addressing challenges in fault tolerance and time efficiency. Our research prioritizes the identification of EC schemes that optimize both factors specifically for OpenStack Swift cloud storage systems. Among various EC techniques including XOR-based Erasure Codes [15], Fountain Codes [16], Luby Transform (LT) Codes [17], and Reed–Solomon Codes, we select RS Codes for benchmarking due to their known limitations in fault tolerance, inefficiency with large datasets, and error correction capability. Our study focuses on experimenting with diverse datasets to benchmark EC schemes within the Object Storage System context. This involves selecting the most suitable EC technique, setting up an experimental test environment, and carefully curating datasets for thorough analysis and comparison.

To address the existing research gap, our benchmarking framework focuses on addressing challenges related to time efficiency in cloud-based object storage systems. It evaluates I/O activity key metrics 1 such as upload, download, deletion, waiting times, and fault tolerance, with a specific emphasis on assessing the performance of erasure coding techniques. Moreover, our contributions include in-depth time efficiency analysis, fault tolerance exploration, dataset creation, systematic review of existing studies, and identification of opportunities for improvement in EC for cloud-based object storage systems.

Based on our study, we make the following contributions to this paper:

*   •
We investigate the time efficiency of RS Codes (RS (5+3), RS (10+4), RS (7+5)) in OpenStack Swift Object Storage, evaluating upload, download, deletion, and waiting times to provide comparative insights.

*   •
Besides, we explore the fault tolerance aspect of EC using the SimEDC simulator and compare fault tolerance results with various schemes to improve reliability.

*   •
Furthermore, our study contributes significantly by introducing a new EC dataset (MCSD-100) [18], tailored specifically for our benchmarking experiments. Additionally, we utilize the established benchmark dataset (COCO-17) [19] for comparison and validation.

*   •
Finally, we provide a systematic theoretical and experimental review alongside existing literature. Our benchmarking validates the novelty of our research, highlighting opportunities to improve time efficiency and fault tolerance in EC.

Section 6 presents experimental results. Section 7 compares findings with related studies, discusses limitations, and suggests future work. Finally, Section 8 concludes the paper.
