                            Abstract
Most of the data in large-scale storage clusters is erasure
coded. At exascale, optimizing erasure codes for low storage
overhead, efficient reconstruction, and easy deployment is of
critical importance. Locally recoverable codes (LRCs) have
deservedly gained central importance in this field, because
they can balance many of these requirements. In our work
we study wide LRCs; LRCs with large number of blocks per
stripe and low storage overhead. These codes are a natural
next step for practitioners to unlock higher storage savings,
